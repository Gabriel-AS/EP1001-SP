2D CNC Machining

GCode from Fusion 360

There are a few ways to generate Gcode for the massive 2D CNC down at FabLab, such as from Vcarve Pro.
I prefer working with Fusion 360 directly. For my CNC project, I want to construct a stand for a prop
rifle and a bayonet. This is the model I built in Fusion 360, as always, link to file on the homepage.

Now make sure to lay the parts flat like this

One important thing to do, is to keep all the material widths, and the slot sizes on parametric. This
makes it much easier to edit the model to account for inconsistencies in the material thickness.
Under modify, change parameters.

You will arrive at this menu, notice that the material thickness I selected is 15mm, which is the typical
thickness on most of the scrap plywood sheets.

Also, because the CNC machine uses a rotary bit, it does not cope well with Right angle cuts. Which is why
we apply these cuts known as Dogbones into the right angle cuts. Without them, the Right angle cuts come out
with a small fillet because of the rotary bit. I am using a dogbone Add in I found on the Autodesk app store.
